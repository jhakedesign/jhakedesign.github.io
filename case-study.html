Case Study · Brand & Curriculum Design

Project
CREATE

A design-education curriculum built from the ground up for high school students across southern Louisiana — teaching real Adobe workflows through real client briefs.

Role
Creative Manager
Timeline
2017 – 2020
Client
PerXuade / XP Synergy
Deliverables
Curriculum, Brand Kit, Print

The Brief

Teach real design, not textbook design.

XP Synergy needed a program that could take students with zero design background and get them producing client-ready work within a semester. The existing curriculum leaned too academic — theory-heavy, software-light, and disconnected from what an actual studio does day to day.

I was brought on to rebuild the program from the syllabus up: pairing every lesson with a live local-business brief, so students learned Illustrator and InDesign the same way a junior designer would — under a deadline, with a client waiting.

The System

A brand kit built for a classroom, not a boardroom.

Every unit shipped with its own mini brand system — type pairing, a locked color story, and a set of layout grids — so students could focus on craft instead of guessing at rules. The kit doubled as the visual identity for the program itself, right down to the folder templates students turned work in with.

How It Came Together

Process

Audit

Sat in on existing classes and interviewed instructors to find where students were losing interest and where the software was getting in the way.

Structure

Mapped a semester into six client-style units, each ending in a deliverable a real business could actually use.

Build

Designed the brand kit, lesson templates, and grading rubrics alongside a team of three instructors.

Run & Refine

Piloted the program, sat in on every critique, and rewrote the weakest units before the next cohort started.

Outcomes

Results

students completed the program across three cohorts.

14

local businesses received finished design work from student briefs.
